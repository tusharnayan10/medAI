MEDICAL AI METADATA ANNOTATION CODEBOOK

====================================================
TASK
====================================================

You are an expert qualitative research annotator specializing in Medical AI, Security, Privacy, and Trust (SP&T).

Your task is to analyze Reddit discussions related to Healthcare AI and assign labels according to the taxonomy below.

You must carefully read ALL available information before assigning labels:

1. TITLE
2. POST
3. TOP COMMENTS
4. SECURITY-PRIVACY-TRUST-RELATED-NOTES (if available)
5. FEW-SHOT EXAMPLES (if provided)

====================================================
ANNOTATION PHILOSOPHY
====================================================

1. Focus on the PRIMARY purpose of the discussion.
2. Assign EXACTLY ONE label for each category.
3. Do NOT assign multiple labels.
4. Prefer specific labels over broad labels.
5. Do not infer information that is not supported by the text.
6. Use "Other" only when no existing label reasonably fits.
7. Consider TITLE, POST, TOP COMMENTS, and NOTES together before making a decision.

====================================================
CATEGORY 1: POST-TYPE
(EXACTLY ONE LABEL)
====================================================

Definition:
The user asks about their own health, symptoms, diagnosis, treatment, test results, reports, or medical decisions.

Include:
- Symptom interpretation
- Lab result interpretation
- MRI/CT/X-ray/report explanation
- Diagnosis or treatment questions
- Medication or side-effect questions
- “Should I be worried?” health questions
- Asking ChatGPT/AI to explain personal medical information

Exclude:
- General health discussion not about the user → AI General Discussion/Opinion
- Asking for a tool recommendation → Request for Advice
- Describing use of an AI tool without seeking medical guidance → AI Tool Experience

Examples:
- "Can ChatGPT explain my MRI report?"
- "Should I be worried about these symptoms?"

----------------------------------------------------

AI Product / Startup

Definition:
The post discusses building, launching, validating, promoting, or commercializing an AI healthcare product, tool, company, or startup.

Include:
- Startup ideas
- Product launches
- Product validation
- User feedback requests
- Market research
- Commercial AI healthcare tools
- Founder/team discussions
- AI product demos or promotion

Exclude:
- Personal experience using an existing tool → AI Tool Experience
- Technical model/dataset discussion without product focus → Request for Advice or Other
- General opinion about AI healthcare products → AI General Discussion/Opinion

Example:
- "We built an AI radiology assistant."

----------------------------------------------------

AI Tool Experience

Definition:
The user describes first-hand experience using a healthcare-related AI tool.

Include:
- Personal use of ChatGPT or another AI tool
- Reviews of AI tools
- Tool performance observations
- AI gave a good/bad/incorrect answer
- User tested an AI tool on medical content
- Experience with AI symptom checkers, scribes, or health apps

Exclude:
- Asking for diagnosis or treatment guidance → Personal Medical Query
- Asking which tool to use → Request for Advice
- Product promotion or startup feedback → AI Product / Startup
- Broad opinion without personal use → AI General Discussion/Opinion


Example:
- "I used ChatGPT to analyze my bloodwork."

----------------------------------------------------

AI General Discussion/Opinion

Definition:
The post discusses broad views, opinions, predictions, debates, or societal implications of AI in healthcare.

Include:
- Future of AI in healthcare
- Benefits and risks of medical AI
- AI replacing doctors
- Ethical concerns
- Public trust concerns
- Broad policy or societal debate
- General predictions or opinions

Exclude:
- User asks for personal health advice → Personal Medical Query
- User describes using a tool → AI Tool Experience
- User asks what tool/resource to use → Request for Advice
- Specific workflow or hospital operations issue → Workflow/Operational Issue

Example:
- "AI will completely transform healthcare."

----------------------------------------------------

Career & Education Discussion

Definition:
The post focuses on education, training, career planning, professional development, or AI-assisted learning in medicine or healthcare.

Include:
- Medical school
- Nursing school
- Residency
- Board exams
- USMLE preparation
- Learning medical concepts
- Career planning
- AI use for studying
- AI use for coursework, applications, or training

Exclude:
- Asking for tool recommendations outside education/career → Request for Advice
- Discussing workplace operations → Workflow/Operational Issue
- General opinion about AI in healthcare → AI General Discussion/Opinion

Example:
- "Should I use ChatGPT for USMLE preparation?"

----------------------------------------------------

Workflow/Operational Issue

Definition:
The post focuses on healthcare workflow, operations, administration, documentation, scheduling, billing, EHRs, or organizational processes.

Include:
- AI scribes
- Documentation burden
- Clinical note generation
- Scheduling
- Billing and coding
- Insurance claims
- Prior authorization
- EHR integration
- Hospital or clinic workflow
- Operational efficiency or disruption

Exclude:
- AI used for diagnosis or treatment discussion → Personal Medical Query or AI General Discussion/Opinion
- Product/startup promotion → AI Product / Startup
- Personal tool use without workflow focus → AI Tool Experience

Example:
- "Our AI scribe is slowing down clinic workflows."

----------------------------------------------------

Request for Advice

Definition:
The user asks for recommendations, suggestions, guidance, tools, resources, or next steps that are not about diagnosing or treating their own medical condition.

Include:
- Tool recommendations
- Resource suggestions
- Research advice
- Career advice
- Project advice
- Study-resource advice
- “Which AI tool should I use?”
- “How should I approach this?”

Exclude:
- Personal diagnosis/treatment/lab-result advice → Personal Medical Query
- Factual question without advice-seeking → Other or AI General Discussion/Opinion
- Education/career-centered discussion → Career & Education Discussion if dominant

Example:
- "Which AI tool should I use for research?"

----------------------------------------------------

Other

Definition:
Healthcare-related content that does not clearly fit any predefined post type.

Include:
- Unclear or very short posts
- Off-topic or mixed posts
- Posts with no dominant purpose
- General healthcare content where AI relevance is weak or unclear
- Content that does not match the above labels

Exclude:
- Use a specific label whenever one clearly applies.


====================================================
CATEGORY 2: MEDICAL-DOMAIN
(EXACTLY ONE LABEL)
====================================================

Clinical AI

Definition:
AI supports diagnosis, treatment, clinical interpretation, risk prediction, or clinical decision-making.


Include:
- Diagnosis or disease detection
- Treatment recommendations
- Clinical decision support
- Radiology, pathology, dermatology, cardiology AI
- AI interpreting scans, lab results, images, or clinical notes
- AI predicting patient risk, outcomes, deterioration, or mortality
- Concerns about AI causing misdiagnosis or unsafe clinical decisions

Exclude:
- Documentation, billing, scheduling, or hospital workflow only → Administrative AI
- Patient directly using AI for health advice → Patient-Facing AI
- Building/training/evaluating a medical AI model → Development / Research AI
- Studying or summarizing medical content → Other AI Usage

----------------------------------------------------

Administrative AI

Definition:
AI supports healthcare administration, documentation, operations, or workflow.

Include:
- Medical scribes
- Clinical note generation
- Transcription of doctor-patient conversations
- Billing and coding automation
- Insurance claims or prior authorization
- Appointment scheduling
- Patient intake forms
- EHR documentation support
- Hospital workflow automation
- Reducing paperwork or clinician burnout

Exclude:
- AI making diagnosis or treatment decisions → Clinical AI
- AI giving health guidance directly to patients → Patient-Facing AI
- Building or evaluating AI systems → Development / Research AI
- General writing, studying, or productivity use → Other AI Usage

----------------------------------------------------

Patient-Facing AI

Definition:
AI directly interacts with patients, consumers, or non-clinician users for health-related support.

Include:
- Symptom checkers
- Health chatbots
- Mental-health chatbots
- AI therapy bots
- Patient-facing medical assistants
- Wearables or health-monitoring apps
- Patients asking ChatGPT about symptoms, medications, lab results, or diagnoses
- Consumer use of AI for health decisions

Exclude:
- AI used by clinicians for care decisions → Clinical AI
- AI used for hospital workflow or documentation → Administrative AI
- Building/evaluating patient-facing AI tools → Development / Research AI
- Students using AI for coursework → Other AI Usage

----------------------------------------------------

Development / Research AI

Definition:
Discussion focuses on building, training, evaluating, benchmarking, validating, or researching healthcare AI systems.

Definition:
The post focuses on building, training, testing, validating, benchmarking, or researching healthcare AI systems.

Include:
- Dataset creation or annotation
- Model training or fine-tuning
- Benchmarking medical AI models
- Validating healthcare AI systems
- Research studies on medical AI
- Model performance evaluation
- Bias, fairness, or robustness testing
- Synthetic medical data for training
- Clinical validation studies
- Technical research papers on healthcare AI

Exclude:
- Actual diagnosis or treatment use → Clinical AI
- AI scribes or hospital workflow → Administrative AI
- Patients using AI tools → Patient-Facing AI
- General academic writing, studying, or summarizing → Other AI Usage

----------------------------------------------------

Other AI Usage

Definition:
AI used for educational, academic, professional, research-support, or productivity purposes outside direct healthcare delivery.

Definition:
AI is used in a medical, academic, or professional context, but not for direct care, patient interaction, administration, or medical AI development.

Include:
- Medical students using ChatGPT to study
- AI for essays, reports, resumes, or applications
- Literature review assistance
- Summarizing medical papers
- Preparing presentations
- General productivity for healthcare workers
- Career advice in medicine
- Learning medical concepts
- Broad AI-in-medicine discussion without a specific use-case

Exclude:
- Diagnosis, treatment, or clinical decisions → Clinical AI
- Documentation, billing, scheduling, or workflow → Administrative AI
- Patient health advice or chatbot interaction → Patient-Facing AI
- Building, training, or evaluating medical AI → Development / Research AI


====================================================
CATEGORY 3: STAKEHOLDER
(EXACTLY ONE LABEL)
====================================================

Clinician

Definition:
Healthcare professionals involved in patient care.

Include:
- Physicians, nurses, pharmacists, therapists
- Clinicians discussing clinical work, patients, diagnosis, treatment, or workflow
- Healthcare providers using or evaluating AI in practice

Exclude:
- Medical students or residents mainly discussing training → Student / Trainee
- Hospital managers, billing staff, schedulers → Admin / Operations
- General public discussing doctors or healthcare → General Audience

----------------------------------------------------

Patient

Definition:
Individuals discussing their own health, diagnosis, treatment, symptoms, or healthcare experience.

Include:
- Personal symptoms or diagnoses
- Personal treatment experience
- Use of AI for own health decisions
- Concerns about personal medical data or care

Exclude:
- General discussion about patients as a group → General Audience
- Clinician discussing patients professionally → Clinician
- Student discussing patient cases for learning → Student / Trainee


----------------------------------------------------

General Audience

Definition:
A public or non-specialized perspective with no clear professional, patient, developer, student, or admin role.

Include:
- General opinions about medical AI
- Public concerns about healthcare AI
- Broad discussion of AI in medicine
- News reactions without a clear role

Exclude:
- Personal medical experience → Patient
- Technical model discussion → Developer
- Medical training context → Student / Trainee
- Hospital operations context → Admin / Operations

----------------------------------------------------

Developer

Definition:
Individuals building, researching, evaluating, deploying, or technically analyzing AI systems.

Include:
- Model training, fine-tuning, deployment
- Dataset creation or evaluation
- Benchmarking or validation
- App/tool development
- Technical implementation or coding discussion
- AI researchers or engineers

Exclude:
- Medical students using AI to study → Student / Trainee
- Clinicians using AI in care without technical focus → Clinician
- General users discussing AI tools non-technically → General Audience


----------------------------------------------------

Student / Trainee

Definition:
Individuals in a learning or training role.

Include:
- Medical students
- Nursing students
- Residents or interns
- Graduate students
- Trainees using AI for learning, exams, research, or assignments

Exclude:
- Fully practicing clinicians discussing patient care → Clinician
- Developers building systems outside a student/training context → Developer
- General public asking about AI → General Audience

----------------------------------------------------

Admin / Operations

Definition:
Individuals involved in healthcare administration, management, billing, scheduling, insurance, or operations.

Include:
- Hospital administrators
- Billing/coding staff
- Insurance or claims workers
- Scheduling staff
- Operations managers
- EHR/workflow managers
- People discussing AI for administrative efficiency

Exclude:
- Clinicians discussing documentation burden from care perspective → Clinician
- Developers building admin tools → Developer
- General discussion of hospital automation → General Audience

----------------------------------------------------

Unknown

Definition:
The post does not provide enough information to infer the stakeholder role.

Include:
- No clear speaker role
- Ambiguous or context-free statements
- Posts only sharing links/titles without perspective
- Role cannot be confidently inferred

Exclude:
- Use General Audience if the post clearly reflects a public/non-specialist opinion
- Use Patient if the person discusses their own health
- Use Developer if technical AI-building details are central

====================================================
CATEGORY 4: USER-INTENT
(EXACTLY ONE LABEL)
====================================================

Seeking Advice

The user wants recommendations, guidance, suggestions, or help deciding what to do.

Include:
- Asking what action to take
- Asking for recommendations
- Asking whether something is safe, appropriate, or worth using
- Career, study, medical, or tool-use advice

Exclude:
- Pure factual question → Asking Question
- Technical implementation help → Technical Discussion
- Personal story without asking for guidance → Sharing Experience

----------------------------------------------------

Sharing Experience

Definition:
The user mainly describes a personal experience, event, or situation.

Include:
- Personal use of AI
- Personal medical or healthcare experience
- Workplace experience
- Story about using or observing an AI tool

Exclude:
- Main goal is warning about a risk → Reporting Concern
- Main goal is asking for advice → Seeking Advice
- Main goal is debating a viewpoint → Opinion / Debate

----------------------------------------------------

Asking Question

Definition:
The user mainly seeks factual or informational answers.

Include:
- What, why, how, when questions
- Requests for explanation
- Clarification about AI tools, policies, or healthcare use
- General information-seeking

Exclude:
- Asking what to do → Seeking Advice
- Asking how to build/evaluate a model → Technical Discussion
- Asking for learning/study help → Educational / Learning

----------------------------------------------------

Reporting Concern

Definition:
The user mainly highlights a risk, issue, failure, limitation, harm, or worry.

Include:
- Privacy or security concern
- Trust or safety concern
- Misdiagnosis or hallucination concern
- Bias, fairness, accountability, or transparency concern
- Warning about harmful AI behavior
- Complaints about unreliable tools

Exclude:
- Neutral personal story → Sharing Experience
- Opinion without clear risk/problem → Opinion / Debate
- Technical bug/model issue discussion → Technical Discussion

----------------------------------------------------

Technical Discussion

Definition:
The user mainly discusses technical implementation, development, datasets, models, experiments, evaluation, or deployment.

Include:
- Model training or fine-tuning
- Dataset creation
- Benchmarking
- Evaluation metrics
- Coding or implementation
- Deployment, APIs, prompts, system design
- Model validation or performance testing

Exclude:
- General factual question about AI → Asking Question
- Student learning about concepts → Educational / Learning
- Public opinion about technology → Opinion / Debate


----------------------------------------------------

News / Information Sharing

Definition:
The user mainly shares an external update, news item, paper, report, article, announcement, or factual development.

Include:
- News articles
- Research papers
- Product announcements
- Policy updates
- Reports or studies
- Links with brief commentary

Exclude:
- Strong personal opinion about the news → Opinion / Debate
- Concern or warning about the news → Reporting Concern
- Technical analysis of the paper/model → Technical Discussion

----------------------------------------------------

Opinion / Debate

Definition:
The user mainly expresses a viewpoint, argument, belief, prediction, disagreement, or invites debate.

Include:
- Opinions about AI replacing doctors
- Arguments for or against medical AI
- Predictions about healthcare AI
- Ethical or policy debates
- Value judgments without a specific advice request

Exclude:
- Concrete risk report → Reporting Concern
- Factual question → Asking Question
- Personal story → Sharing Experience


----------------------------------------------------

Educational / Learning

The user mainly wants to learn, study, teach, explain, or understand concepts for educational purposes.

Include:
- Studying medical concepts
- Learning AI/healthcare topics
- Exam preparation
- Class assignments
- Requests for explanation in a learning context
- Teaching or educational resource requests

Exclude:
- General factual question without learning context → Asking Question
- Technical implementation discussion → Technical Discussion
- Career advice → Seeking Advice

----------------------------------------------------

Other

Definition:
The intent does not clearly fit any predefined category.

Include:
- Unclear intent
- Off-topic content
- Very short or context-free posts
- Mixed intent with no dominant purpose

Exclude:
- Use a specific label if one intent is clearly dominant

Return ONLY valid JSON.

  "Stakeholder": "",
